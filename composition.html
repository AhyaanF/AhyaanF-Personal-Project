Video Composition

Composition Rules

Learn the fundamental principles of visual composition in video.

Basic Composition Guidelines


1. Rule of Thirds
   - Divide frame into 3x3 grid
   - Place subjects on intersections
   - Horizon on horizontal lines
   - Leave lead room for movement

2. Leading Lines
   - Use natural lines
   - Guide viewer's eye
   - Create depth
   - Frame within frame

3. Symmetry/Balance
   - Center composition
   - Equal visual weight
   - Mirror images
   - Geometric patterns

4. Depth Layers
   - Foreground elements
   - Mid-ground subject
   - Background context
   - Create dimensionality

Shot Types

Understanding different shot types and their storytelling impact.

Common Shot Types


1. Wide Shot (WS)
   - Establish location
   - Show environment
   - Full body in frame
   - Context and scale

2. Medium Shot (MS)
   - Waist up framing
   - Social distance
   - Balanced detail
   - Conversation scenes

3. Close-Up (CU)
   - Face or detail
   - Emotional impact
   - Focus attention
   - Reveal texture

4. Extreme Close-Up (ECU)
   - Specific details
   - Abstract views
   - High impact
   - Dramatic effect

Movement and Framing

Dynamic Composition


1. Camera Movement
   - Motivated motion
   - Smooth transitions
   - Follow action
   - Reveal information

2. Subject Movement
   - Lead room
   - Exit frame space
   - Movement direction
   - Screen direction

3. Frame Dynamics
   - Negative space
   - Visual tension
   - Balance vs. imbalance
   - Frame within frame

4. Aspect Ratios
   - 16:9 Standard
   - 2.35:1 Cinematic
   - 1:1 Social media
   - 9:16 Mobile

Practice Exercise

Create a sequence that demonstrates:

  • Rule of thirds application
  • Various shot types
  • Dynamic movement
  • Depth and layering

Example Sequence:

1. Opening Shot
   - Wide establishing shot
   - Rule of thirds horizon
   - Leading lines to subject
   - Three depth layers

2. Character Introduction
   - Medium shot on thirds
   - Eye-line space
   - Background separation
   - Natural framing

3. Detail Sequence
   - Close-up of action
   - Balanced composition
   - Motivated movement
   - Focus pull for depth

4. Closing Shot
   - Dynamic movement
   - Reveal new information
   - Strong foreground element
   - Resolution of action
