Camera Basics

Understanding Your Camera

Learn the fundamental camera settings and controls that are essential for quality video production.

Basic Camera Settings


1. Resolution Settings
   - 4K (3840 x 2160)
   - 1080p (1920 x 1080)
   - 720p (1280 x 720)

2. Frame Rates
   - 24fps (cinematic)
   - 30fps (standard)
   - 60fps (smooth motion)
   - 120fps+ (slow motion)

3. Shutter Speed
   - Basic rule: 2x frame rate
   - 1/50 for 24fps
   - 1/60 for 30fps
   - 1/120 for 60fps

4. Aperture (f-stop)
   - f/1.8 - f/2.8: Shallow depth
   - f/4 - f/5.6: Medium depth
   - f/8 - f/16: Deep depth

Camera Movement

Master the basic camera movements that form the foundation of video cinematography.

Essential Camera Moves


1. Static Shots
   - Tripod setup
   - Level horizon
   - Rule of thirds

2. Pan Movement
   - Horizontal rotation
   - Smooth motion
   - Start and end points

3. Tilt Movement
   - Vertical rotation
   - Controlled speed
   - Subject tracking

4. Dolly Movement
   - Forward/backward
   - Constant speed
   - Focus maintenance

Composition Techniques

Learn the fundamental rules of composition to create visually appealing shots.

Composition Guidelines


1. Rule of Thirds
   - Divide frame into 3x3 grid
   - Place subjects at intersections
   - Balance negative space

2. Leading Lines
   - Use natural lines
   - Direct viewer attention
   - Create depth

3. Headroom
   - Proper subject placement
   - Eye-line consideration
   - Frame balance

4. Shot Types
   - Wide shot (establishes scene)
   - Medium shot (context)
   - Close-up (details)
   - Extreme close-up (emphasis)

Practice Exercise

Create a short sequence demonstrating:

  • Proper camera settings for indoor shooting
  • Three different types of camera movements
  • Application of composition rules
  • Various shot types

Sample Sequence:

1. Opening Shot
   - Wide shot of room
   - Static on tripod
   - Rule of thirds composition
   Settings: 1080p, 24fps, 1/50 shutter, f/4

2. Subject Introduction
   - Medium shot
   - Slow pan from left to right
   - Leading lines with room features
   Settings: Same as above, adjust aperture if needed

3. Detail Emphasis
   - Close-up shot
   - Shallow depth of field
   - Centered composition
   Settings: Open up to f/2.8 for shallow depth

4. Movement Sequence
   - Start wide
   - Dolly in to medium shot
   - End with tilt up
   Settings: Ensure smooth movement, maintain focus
